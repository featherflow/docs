Every funding round announcement is a green light. The company just validated their model enough to attract investment. They're entering a growth phase. They have budget. They have urgency. They're hiring.

For B2B sellers, a funding event at an ICP-fit company is one of the clearest buying signals available.

---

## Funding Stages and Relevance

| Stage | Typical Size | What It Signals | Outbound Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-seed** | < $1M | Idea stage, tiny team | Usually too early unless you have a startup-focused product |
| **Seed** | $1M–$5M | Building MVP, hiring founding team | Monitor; outreach if they're hiring sales |
| **Series A** | $5M–$20M | Proven product, building go-to-market | **High** — first serious GTM investment |
| **Series B** | $15M–$50M | Scaling what works, professionalizing | **Very high** — investing in infrastructure and efficiency |
| **Series C+** | $50M+ | Expanding market, enterprise motion | High — large budget, complex procurement |
| **Growth/PE** | $100M+ | Late-stage optimization | Medium — longer cycles, more stakeholders |

Series A and B are the sweet spot for most B2B outbound tools. Companies have proven traction but are still building the infrastructure to scale.

## Signal Anatomy: What to Extract from a Funding Announcement

Every funding announcement contains more than just the dollar amount. Here's what to extract:

**Company signals:**
- Total raised (context for budget scale)
- Lead investor (tier of investor signals company quality)
- Board changes (new investors often bring in operators as advisors)

**Growth signals:**
- Stated use of funds: "expand sales team" vs. "product development" vs. "international expansion"
- Headcount plans mentioned in the press release
- New markets or products mentioned

**Timing signals:**
- How long since the last round (burn rate indicator)
- How many rounds total (stage of company journey)

```
Analyze this funding announcement for outbound signals:

Company: {{company_name}}
Announcement: {{press_release_or_news_snippet}}

Extract:
1. Round size
2. Funding stage (seed/series-a/series-b/series-c/growth)
3. Stated use of funds (especially: team expansion, sales/marketing investment)
4. Key investor names
5. Any GTM or product expansion signals
6. Outbound relevance: high/medium/low (based on whether stated use of funds involves sales/growth)
7. Optimal outreach window: how many days from announcement?

Return as JSON.
```

## Detecting Funding Events

Sources for funding signal detection:

| Source | Update Frequency | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| **Crunchbase** | Near real-time | Excellent for US, good globally |
| **PitchBook** | Real-time | Best for institutional rounds, expensive |
| **TechCrunch** | Daily | High-profile rounds, editorial coverage |
| **LinkedIn company updates** | Variable | Self-reported, often earlier than official sources |
| **SEC EDGAR** | Filing-based | US companies only, some delay |
| **Google Alerts** | Variable | Good for news coverage of rounds |

For automated detection, Crunchbase API is the standard. Set up webhook triggers for new funding events matching your ICP criteria (industry + stage + geography).

## Growth Signals Beyond Funding

Funding is the most obvious growth signal but not the only one. These are equally actionable:

| Signal | Source | Outreach Angle |
|---|---|---|
| **Rapid headcount growth** (>30% in 12mo) | LinkedIn company page | "You're scaling fast — how are you building the GTM infrastructure to support it?" |
| **New market entry** | Press release, LinkedIn | "Saw you're expanding to [market] — outbound infrastructure is usually the first bottleneck in a new geo" |
| **Strategic partnership announcement** | PR, LinkedIn | "The [Partner] announcement must be driving a lot of new inbound — how are you turning that into outbound pipeline?" |
| **New product launch** | Product Hunt, company blog | "New product = new ICP = new outbound motion. Here's how teams handle this." |
| **IPO filing** | SEC EDGAR | High-urgency, enterprise motion — needs senior outreach |

## Funding-Triggered Email Template

```
Write a funding-triggered cold email:

Prospect: {{first_name}} {{last_name}}, {{job_title}} at {{company_name}}
Funding event: {{funding_stage}} round of {{round_size}}
Stated use of funds: {{use_of_funds}}
Days since announcement: {{days_since}}

Requirements:
- Reference the specific funding event (not generic congratulations)
- Connect their growth phase to a specific problem we solve (outbound infrastructure doesn't scale automatically with headcount)
- Keep it under 80 words
- CTA: Specific ask — "show you what this looks like for a team at your stage"
- Tone: Collegial, not salesy

Return ONLY the email body.
```
