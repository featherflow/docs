## Why This Module Exists

Most SDRs and founders skip email infrastructure. They set up a new domain,
load a list, and start sending — then spend weeks wondering why their emails
land in spam. By the time they diagnose the problem, the domain reputation
is damaged and recovery takes months.

This module is the one you wish you'd done first.

## The Three Non-Negotiables

<Steps>
  <Step title="SPF (Sender Policy Framework)">
    A DNS record that specifies which mail servers are authorized to send
    email on behalf of your domain. Without it, receiving mail servers
    have no way to verify your identity.

    **How to set it up:** Add a TXT record to your DNS:
    `v=spf1 include:youremailprovider.com ~all`

    Most email providers (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, etc.) give you
    the exact record to add during setup.
  </Step>
  <Step title="DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)">
    A cryptographic signature attached to every email you send.
    It proves the email wasn't modified in transit and comes from a
    legitimate source.

    **How to set it up:** Your email provider generates a DKIM key pair.
    You add the public key as a TXT record in DNS. The provider handles
    signing automatically after that.
  </Step>
  <Step title="DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication)">
    A policy that tells receiving servers what to do when SPF or DKIM fails.
    Without DMARC, a failing SPF check might still let your email through —
    or might silently drop it with no feedback.

    **Recommended starting policy:** `v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:your@email.com`
    The `p=none` policy monitors without blocking — you'll get reports showing
    whether your authentication is working before enforcing stricter rules.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Domain Warmup

A new sending domain has no reputation. ISPs treat it as a potential spam source
until it builds a history of legitimate engagement. Warmup means gradually
increasing your send volume over 4–6 weeks.

**Warmup schedule:**
| Week | Daily sends |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | 10–20 |
| Week 2 | 30–50 |
| Week 3 | 75–100 |
| Week 4 | 150–200 |
| Week 5+ | Scale up |

Use a warmup tool (Mailreach, Warmbox, or your sending platform's built-in warmup)
to automate this. Warmup tools send emails to real inboxes in their network and
simulate engagement, building your reputation without risking real prospects.
