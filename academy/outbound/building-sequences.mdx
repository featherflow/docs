## What a Good Sequence Looks Like

A sequence is a series of coordinated touchpoints across channels, spaced
deliberately, each one building on the last. The goal is not to overwhelm —
it's to give the prospect multiple opportunities to respond at the moment
it's convenient for them.

A standard 3-touch sequence that works:

| Touch | Channel | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Email | Day 0 | First contact — personalized, specific, one CTA |
| 2 | LinkedIn | Day 3 | Connection request with a short context note |
| 3 | Email | Day 7 | Follow-up referencing the LinkedIn connection |

After 3 touches with no response, move on. Pestering someone past 3 touches
damages your brand, not just your reply rate.

## Building the Sequence in Bitscale

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare your grid">
    Your grid should have: Company Name, First Name, Last Name, Email,
    LinkedIn URL, and the personalization data you'll use in your copy
    (company description, recent news, job posting, etc.).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add an Email Generation column">
    Click **+ Add Column** → **Text Model** → connect to OpenAI or Claude.
    Write your prompt: "Write a 3-sentence cold email to {first_name} at
    {company_name}. Reference {personalization_hook}. End with one specific
    question." The prompt is a template — Bitscale fills in the variables
    from your other columns.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a Subject Line column">
    Separate column, same setup. Prompt: "Write 3 subject line options for
    this email: {email_body}. Keep each under 50 characters. No clickbait."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export to your sending tool">
    Once the grid is enriched and copy is generated, export to Instantly,
    Smartlead, Lemlist, or your preferred tool. Map your columns to their
    fields: Email → To, Subject → Subject, Email Body → Body.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## LinkedIn Coordination

The LinkedIn step isn't automated through Bitscale directly (LinkedIn
restricts this). Instead, use Bitscale to generate the connection note
copy — a personalized 200-character message for each prospect — and
export it alongside the contact list for manual or semi-automated sending
via a LinkedIn tool.
