# Building Sequences

The best cold emails don't sound like cold emails.

They sound like a message from someone who did their homework — someone who knows what's happening at your company, why it's relevant to them, and what a good next step looks like. The trick is that they didn't do the homework manually. They used data to make automation feel personal.

That's what we're building in this module.

## The Signal-Value-Close Framework

Most outbound sequences fail because every touch tries to do the same thing: get a meeting. Good sequences do three different jobs across three different touches.

| Touch | Timing | Job | Approach |
|-------|--------|-----|---------|
| **1 — Signal** | Day 0 | Establish relevance | Reference a real, specific trigger |
| **2 — Value** | Day 3 | Prove you're worth talking to | Share a concrete result, not a feature list |
| **3 — Close** | Day 7 | Respect their time | Low-friction, keep the door open |

Each touch has one job. The signal touch gets their attention. The value touch earns credibility. The close touch respects their inbox.

---

## Touch 1: The Signal Email

The opener is the make-or-break moment. One sentence that makes the prospect think "how did they know that?" — followed by a clear, low-friction ask.

**Anatomy of a Signal email:**

```
Subject: [signal-based, 5–7 words, no spam triggers]

[Signal-based opener — one sentence referencing a real trigger]

[Bridge — one sentence connecting the signal to their likely problem]

[Value statement — one sentence on the outcome you deliver]

[CTA — one sentence, specific, easy to say yes to]

[Name]
```

**Example:**

> Subject: scaling your SDR team
>
> Saw you're hiring 4 SDRs at Acme — most teams in that phase spend 30% of their ramp budget on manual data work that Bitscale automates in 60 seconds.
>
> Worth a quick look?
>
> [Name]

That's it. 3 sentences plus a subject line. Under 50 words. No pitch deck. No feature list. Just a relevant observation and a low-friction ask.

---

## Touch 2: The Value Email

If Touch 1 got an open but no reply, Touch 2 drops proof.

```
Subject: Re: [original subject — keep the thread]

Quick follow-up.

[Specific outcome a similar company got — use a real customer if possible]

[One sentence on why this is relevant to them]

Worth 15 minutes to see if it makes sense for Acme?

[Name]
```

**Key principle:** Never repeat Touch 1. Touch 2 should add new information — specifically, a concrete result that makes the prospect think "that could be us."

---

## Touch 3: The Close

Touch 3 is not another pitch. It's a graceful exit that keeps the door open.

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Closing the loop here — completely understand if the timing isn't right.

Happy to reconnect in Q3 if anything changes. Just reply and I'll set something up.

[Name]
```

That's it. No follow-up on the follow-up. Respect their inbox. Deals that come back from a Touch 3 close often convert faster than ones that replied to Touch 1 — because the prospect made a decision, not a reflex response.

## The AI Opener Prompt (Copy-Paste Ready)

```
You are writing the opening line of a cold email to {{first_name}}, {{title}} at {{company}}.

They are currently hiring {{sdr_hiring_count}} SDRs based on their job postings.

Write one sentence that:
- References this specific hiring signal naturally (not creepily)
- Is under 20 words
- Does not use the word "noticed"
- Sounds like a smart colleague wrote it, not a salesperson
- Contains no emojis
```

Swap `{{sdr_hiring_count}}` for whichever enrichment column has your signal. Run the column across all 50 rows in about 15 seconds.

## Adding LinkedIn to Your Sequence

One pattern that consistently lifts reply rates: a LinkedIn touch between Touch 1 and Touch 2.

| Day | Channel | Action |
|-----|---------|--------|
| 0 | Email | Touch 1 (Signal email) |
| 1 | LinkedIn | Connection request — blank or 1-line note |
| 3 | Email | Touch 2 (Value email) |
| 5 | LinkedIn | Short message: reference your email, one question |
| 7 | Email | Touch 3 (Close) |

When Touch 2 arrives after you've connected on LinkedIn, your name is familiar. Familiarity converts.

**AI column for the LinkedIn note:**

```
Write a one-line LinkedIn connection request note to {{first_name}} at {{company}}.

Context: You're reaching out because they're hiring SDRs and you want to connect about GTM automation.

Rules:
- Maximum 15 words
- No pitch
- Sound curious, not salesy
- No emojis
```

One Bitscale grid, two outputs: email opener and LinkedIn note. Same data, zero extra work.
