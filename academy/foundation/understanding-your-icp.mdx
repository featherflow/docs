# Understanding Your ICP

**Bad ICP:** "B2B SaaS companies with 50–500 employees."

That describes roughly 80,000 companies. It gives you zero signal about whether any of them are ready to buy, able to buy, or worth talking to right now.

**Good ICP:** "Series A/B SaaS companies in outbound-heavy verticals (sales tech, HR tech, fintech), 50–200 employees, actively hiring SDRs, using Outreach or Salesloft, VP Sales in seat for less than 18 months."

That describes maybe 2,000 companies — and every single one of them has a reason to care about what you're selling.

The difference is precision. This module teaches you how to get there.

## The Four ICP Dimensions

A precise ICP has four layers. Most teams only define the first one.

### 1. Firmographic

The baseline structural facts about a company. Necessary — but not sufficient.

| Signal | Example | Bitscale Column Type |
|--------|---------|---------------------|
| Industry | SaaS, fintech, e-commerce | Enrichment → Company Industry |
| Headcount | 50–200 employees | Enrichment → Headcount |
| Revenue range | $5M–$50M ARR | Enrichment → Estimated Revenue |
| Funding stage | Series A or B | Enrichment → Funding Data |
| HQ location | US or UK | Enrichment → Company Location |

Firmographic data is table stakes. Everyone has this. Use it to filter *down*, not to select *up*.

### 2. Situational

What's *happening* at this company right now that makes them a fit.

| Signal | What It Means | Bitscale Column Type |
|--------|--------------|---------------------|
| Hiring SDRs | They're scaling outbound | Job Postings enrichment |
| Raised funding in last 90 days | They have budget to spend | Funding Data enrichment |
| New VP Sales just hired | They're rebuilding the team | Job Change Signal |
| Adopted new tech in last 6 months | They're evaluating new tools | Tech Stack enrichment |

Situational signals are your edge. Most competitors can't see them. You can.

### 3. Stakeholder

Not just which company — but *who inside* the company.

| Factor | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Title | VP Sales, Head of Revenue Ops, Director of Demand Gen |
| Seniority | Director and above |
| Tenure in role | Less than 18 months (still making decisions, not yet entrenched) |
| Reports to | CRO or CEO (indicates budget ownership) |

A VP Sales who's been in role for 3 months is 5x more likely to evaluate new tools than one who's been there 4 years with locked-in processes.

### 4. Behavioral

What they've already *done* that signals genuine intent.

| Behavior | Signal Strength | Source |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| Visited your pricing page | Very high | Reverse IP / intent data |
| Downloaded content or attended a webinar | High | Your CRM |
| Mentioned a pain point on LinkedIn | Medium | Social listening |
| Team member left for a competitor | Low | LinkedIn alerts |

Behavioral signals require more infrastructure to capture but convert 3–4x better than firmographic data alone.

<Tip>
  **Quick check:** What's the difference between a firmographic signal and a situational signal? Give an example of each without looking up.
</Tip>

## How the Four Dimensions Map to Bitscale Columns

Your ICP definition maps directly to your grid structure. Here's what a fully-built ICP grid looks like:

```
Column 1:  Company Name          → Text (static)
Column 2:  Domain                → Text (static)
Column 3:  Industry              → Enrichment (Clearbit / Apollo)
Column 4:  Headcount             → Enrichment (Clearbit)
Column 5:  Funding Stage         → Enrichment (Crunchbase)
Column 6:  SDR Hiring Signal     → Enrichment (Job Postings, filter: SDR)
Column 7:  Days Since Funding    → Formula (derived from Column 5 date)
Column 8:  Best Contact          → Enrichment (Apollo, filter: VP Sales)
Column 9:  ICP Score             → AI Column (0–10 score using all signals above)
Column 10: Include in Campaign   → Formula (ICP Score > 7 = TRUE)
```

Column 10 is your filter. Only rows where `Include in Campaign = TRUE` get exported.

## The Calibration Loop

Your ICP is a hypothesis. Treat it like one.

Every two weeks, do this:

1. Pull your last 10 closed-won deals. What firmographic and situational signals were present?
2. Pull your last 10 closed-lost deals. What was different?
3. Update your Bitscale grid to weight the winning signals higher.

Bitscale makes this loop fast because you're adding or removing a column — not rebuilding a spreadsheet from scratch.

<Warning>
  **The trap:** Most teams define their ICP once, put it in a slide deck, and never update it. Your ICP should evolve every quarter as you learn what actually closes. If it hasn't changed in 6 months, you're not learning from your pipeline data.
</Warning>
