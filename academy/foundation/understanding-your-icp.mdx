## The ICP Problem

"SaaS companies in the US" is not an ICP. Neither is "mid-market B2B."
These are categories, not profiles. An ICP needs to be specific enough that
you could look at a company and say in under 10 seconds whether it fits.

The cost of a vague ICP is high. It means:
- You enrich contacts at companies that will never buy
- Your messaging is generic because it has to speak to everyone
- Your reply rates are low because most of the list isn't relevant

A tight ICP fixes all three.

## What Makes a Good ICP

A good ICP has four dimensions:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Firmographic fit">
    Industry, headcount range, geography, revenue band, tech stack.
    The basic profile of the company — who they are.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Situational fit">
    What stage they're in, what problems they likely have right now,
    what events might signal active need (funding, hiring, expansion).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stakeholder fit">
    Which role inside the company owns the problem you solve.
    Job title, seniority, and team size all matter.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Behavioral fit">
    Are they actively signaling a need? Job postings, LinkedIn activity,
    technology changes, funding rounds — these are live signals of fit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Translating ICP into Bitscale Parameters

In Bitscale, your ICP becomes a set of filter parameters in the Find Companies
or Find People data sources. The more specific your ICP, the higher your
signal-to-noise ratio.

Common Bitscale ICP parameters:
- `headcount_min` / `headcount_max`
- `industry` or `keywords`
- `geography` (country, region, city)
- `technologies` (tech stack filters)
- `funding_stage`
- `recent_job_postings` (role-based hiring intent)

## Your Challenge

<Card title="Before moving to F-3" icon="flag-checkered">
  Write out your ICP in one paragraph. Include: industry, headcount range,
  geography, the role you're targeting, and one signal that indicates active need.
  Keep it tight enough that you could reject 80% of companies based on it.
</Card>
