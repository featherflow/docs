# What is GTM Automation?

Your SDR just spent three hours building a prospect list. Here's the uncomfortable truth: by the time they finished, it was already stale.

People change jobs. Companies get acquired. Funding rounds close. The data you manually compiled on Monday is partially wrong by Friday. **GTM Automation doesn't just save time — it solves the staleness problem by making your workflow live, not static.**

## The Real Cost of Manual GTM

The average SDR spends **30% of their time on manual data work.** For a team of five, that's 1.5 full-time salaries going to copy-pasting between tools, looking up companies on LinkedIn, and maintaining spreadsheets that are never fully accurate.

That is not a people problem. It is a systems problem.

GTM Automation is the practice of building systematic workflows that find leads, enrich them with context, and trigger outreach — without a human doing the same task twice.

<Warning>
  **What GTM Automation is not:** It's not a set-and-forget spam machine. The best automated workflows are *more* targeted and *more* personalised than most manual outreach, because they're powered by real-time data signals — not static guesswork from six months ago.
</Warning>

## The GEO Framework

Every workflow you'll ever build in Bitscale maps to one of three layers. Learn this and you'll never need to ask "how do I do X in Bitscale?" — you'll ask "which GEO layer does X live in?" and the answer points you straight to the right tool.

### Generate

This is where you identify *who* belongs in your workflow.

- Companies that raised a Series B in the last 90 days
- Businesses actively hiring SDRs right now
- LinkedIn users who just moved into a VP Sales role
- Accounts that visited your pricing page in the last 7 days

The Generate layer defines your universe. The richer your signal sources, the better your targeting.

### Enrich

Once you have a list, you need context. The Enrich layer answers:
- Who specifically should I contact at this company?
- What tech stack are they on?
- What is their headcount and revenue range?
- What signals tell me they're in-market *right now?*

Bitscale connects to 50+ data providers to answer these questions. You control which providers fire for which rows — and what happens when the first one comes back empty.

### Outreach

With enriched, contextual data, you trigger personalised outreach. Not mail-merge personalisation (`Hi {first_name}`). *Actual* personalisation — copy written by an AI that references exactly what you know about that prospect, sent at exactly the right moment.

## The Four Bitscale Layers

GEO is the framework. These are the four building blocks that make it real inside Bitscale.

| Layer | What It Is | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Sources** | Where your data comes from | LinkedIn Sales Nav, CSV, CRM sync, webhook |
| **Grids** | Where you organise and process data | A grid of 200 target accounts |
| **Columns** | Individual data transformations | Headcount · Hiring signal · AI opener |
| **Exports** | Where enriched data goes next | HubSpot · Outreach · Smartlead · CSV |

Think of a Grid as a spreadsheet where every column is a live API or AI model instead of a formula. Each column asks a question. The answer populates automatically. Change the question, get a different answer — instantly, across every row.

## A Real GEO Workflow (End-to-End)

Here's what this looks like when it's running in production:

**Source:** LinkedIn Sales Navigator search — Series A/B SaaS, 50–300 employees.

**Grid:** 200 companies imported.

**Columns:**
1. Company Name (static)
2. Domain (static)
3. Headcount — enrichment via Clearbit
4. SDR Hiring Signal — enrichment via job postings API (returns count of open SDR roles)
5. Funding Round — enrichment via Crunchbase
6. Best Contact — enrichment via Apollo (finds VP Sales)
7. Email Opener — AI column (generates a signal-based first line)

**Export:** Rows where `SDR Hiring > 0 AND Contact found` → pushed to Outreach sequence automatically.

Total runtime: under 60 seconds. Manually: 2–3 days.

## AI Column Prompt Template

Copy this directly into a Bitscale AI column to generate signal-based email openers:

```
You are writing the opening line of a cold email to {{first_name}}, {{title}} at {{company}}.

Context: This company is currently hiring {{sdr_job_count}} SDRs, suggesting they are scaling their outbound team.

Write a single sentence that references this hiring signal in a natural, non-creepy way.

Rules:
- Maximum 20 words
- Do not use the word "noticed"
- No emojis
- Sound like a human colleague, not a sales robot
```

Replace `{{sdr_job_count}}` with the name of your hiring signal enrichment column.
