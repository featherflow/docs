# Your First Grid

Stop reading. Open Bitscale.

You're going to build a working enrichment workflow by the end of this module. The written content is here as a reference — the real learning happens when you're clicking through it yourself.

**Expected output:** A grid with 25–50 rows, 5 columns (2 static + 3 enrichments), fully populated. You'll screenshot this and submit it as your challenge.

## Before You Start

You'll need:
- Access to a Bitscale workspace
- A CSV file with 25–50 rows: company name + domain (no formatting issues)
- About 20 minutes with no interruptions

<Tip>
  **No data yet?** Use your top 25 target accounts. Google them, copy their domains into a spreadsheet, export as CSV. Takes 5 minutes. Use real companies you'd actually want to reach — it makes the output meaningful and the challenge more useful.
</Tip>

## Step 1: Create the Grid

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click New Grid">
    In the Bitscale dashboard, click **New Grid** in the top left.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name it descriptively">
    Use this naming convention: `[Use Case] — [Target Segment] — [Month Year]`

    Example: `ICP Outbound — Series B SaaS — April 2026`

    You'll thank yourself when you have 30 grids and need to find this one in six months.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and create">
    Click **Create**. You'll land on an empty grid with no columns and no rows. This is correct. Give it five minutes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Step 2: Add Your Static Columns

These two columns are the anchor for everything else. Every enrichment uses Domain as its lookup key.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add Company Name">
    Click **Add Column** → Choose **Text** → Name it `Company Name`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Domain">
    Add Column → Text → Name it `Domain`

    **Why Domain is the most important column:** It's the universal identifier. Every enrichment API — Apollo, Clearbit, Hunter, Crunchbase — accepts domain as input. If you have the domain, you can find everything else.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  **Common mistake #1:** Adding an email column at this stage. Skip it — email comes from your contact enrichment, not from manual input.

  **Common mistake #2:** Entering company websites with `https://` prefixes. Domains only: `acme.com`, not `https://www.acme.com`.
</Warning>

---

## Step 3: Add Your Enrichment Columns

Now the useful part. Each enrichment column fires an API request and returns data automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add Headcount enrichment">
    Add Column → **Enrichment** → Select **Company Headcount**

    The input defaults to `Domain`. Leave it. Click **Save column**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add SDR Hiring Signal">
    Add Column → Enrichment → **Job Postings**

    Configuration:
    - Job title filter: `SDR` or `Sales Development Representative`
    - Time window: Last 30 days
    - Output: Count (returns number of open roles)

    Companies with 2+ open SDR roles are your priority targets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Contact Finder">
    Add Column → Enrichment → **Contact Finder** (or **People Enrichment**)

    Configuration:
    - Title filter: `VP Sales` or `Head of Sales`
    - Seniority: Director+
    - Output: First name, last name, title, LinkedIn URL

    This is the person you'll be reaching out to. Not finding a contact here means you need a different provider or a manual lookup.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Your grid should now have 5 columns:** Company Name · Domain · Headcount · SDR Hiring Signal · Contact

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Run enrichments in order of cost — cheapest first. Headcount is cheap and fast. Contact Finder is slower and more expensive. Run Headcount first — if the company is under 10 employees (too small for your ICP), filter it out before spending credits on the contact lookup.
</Tip>

---

## Step 4: Import Your Rows

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Import Rows">
    In the toolbar, click **Import Rows** → **Import from CSV**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload your file">
    Upload your CSV. Bitscale will detect columns automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map your columns">
    Map your CSV's name column → `Company Name`, domain column → `Domain`. Click **Import**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the import">
    You should see rows populate in 2–3 seconds. Check for domain formatting issues — missing TLDs, extra spaces, or `www.` prefixes. Fix them now before running enrichments or you'll get empty results on clean data.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Step 5: Run Your Enrichments

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run Headcount first">
    Click the **Headcount** column header → **Run All Rows**

    25 rows should complete in under 10 seconds. If a cell returns an error, the domain is wrong or the company is too small/new to appear in the provider's database. You'll learn to handle this properly in F-4.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run SDR Hiring Signal">
    Click **SDR Hiring Signal** → **Run All Rows**

    Expected output: Numbers. 0 = no current openings. 1+ = actively hiring. Companies with 2+ = your warmest targets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run Contact Finder">
    Click **Contact Finder** → **Run All Rows**

    This takes slightly longer (5–20 seconds per row) because it's looking up specific people. Some rows will return empty — that's valid data, not an error. It means there's no VP Sales-equivalent in the provider's database for that company.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Your Completed Grid Should Look Like

| Company Name | Domain | Headcount | SDR Hiring | Contact |
|-------------|--------|-----------|------------|---------|
| Acme Corp | acme.com | 87 | 3 | Sarah Chen, VP Sales |
| Globex Inc | globex.io | 134 | 0 | Michael Torres, Head of Sales |
| Initech | initech.com | 52 | 1 | — (not found) |
| Umbrella | umbrella.co | 12 | 0 | — (too small) |

Rows with `SDR Hiring > 0` are your priority. Rows where Contact is empty need either a different provider or a manual lookup. Rows where Headcount is very small might not be in your ICP at all — you can filter them out.
