## The Three Questions to Ask Every Grid

When you open a freshly enriched grid, run through these three questions before touching a single row:

**1. What's the data quality like?**
Check for null rates. If more than 20% of a column is empty, either the data source doesn't cover that segment well, or your input data (domains, company names) is too messy to enrich reliably.

**2. What do the top-scoring rows have in common?**
Pull the top 20% by ICP score. Look across the columns. Are there patterns beyond what your scoring criteria already capture? New signals emerge here.

**3. What's anomalous?**
Look for rows that score unexpectedly high or low. High scores on companies you know are bad fits mean your criteria are too loose. Low scores on companies you know are strong fits mean you're missing a criterion.

---

## Data Quality Signals to Watch

| Warning Sign | What it Means | How to Fix |
|---|---|---|
| >30% nulls in employee count | Data source doesn't cover this segment | Switch enrichment provider for this column |
| Industry always returns "Technology" | Too broad — needs sub-classification | Add an AI column to re-classify from description |
| Tech stack detection rate <50% | Low coverage for your target segment | Add a secondary detection column (BuiltWith + Wappalyzer) |
| Funding stage mostly null | Private companies — no public funding data | Use headcount growth as proxy signal instead |
| Headcount showing 0 for known large companies | LinkedIn data lag | Cross-reference with Clearbit employee count |

## Interpreting Enrichment Patterns

### Firmographic Clusters

When you sort by industry and employee count together, clusters emerge. A cluster tells you that a specific segment is heavily represented in your dataset — which is either:
- **Good**: your sourcing is already working for this segment
- **Bad**: you're over-indexed on one segment and missing others

### Signal Co-occurrence

When two signals appear together, the combination is usually stronger than either alone. Common powerful combinations:
- Hiring SDRs **and** recently funded → very high intent
- Tech stack includes CRM **and** includes outreach tool → already has infrastructure, likely needs optimization
- Headcount growth >20% **and** no outbound tool detected → classic expansion-stage greenfield

Build a formula column that detects these combinations. The co-occurrence score often beats the individual signal score.

### Negative Signals Hidden in the Data

Data also tells you who to stop pursuing. Look for:
- Companies with 0 headcount growth for 6+ months (stagnant — budget freeze likely)
- Funding rounds older than 24 months with no new round (running out of runway or bootstrapped)
- Tech stack with a direct competitor installed (already solved the problem)

## Building a Data Interpretation Habit

After every grid run, spend 10 minutes doing this:

1. Sort by ICP score descending, read the top 10 manually
2. Sort by ICP score ascending, read the bottom 10 — any surprises?
3. Check null rates for all enrichment columns
4. Add a "Notes" column to any row where the data seems off

This habit surfaces problems early and helps you tune your scoring model over time.
