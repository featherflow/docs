## What You're Looking At

After running enrichment on your grid, you'll have columns filled with
varying types and quality of data. Not all of it is equally useful.
Learning to read data quality quickly is one of the most valuable skills
in the platform.

## Data Quality Signals

<Steps>
  <Step title="Completeness">
    What percentage of rows have a value for each column?
    High-priority columns (email, LinkedIn URL) should be 80%+.
    If you're seeing 40% fill rates, either your source list has quality
    issues or you need to add a fallback enrichment provider.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confidence indicators">
    Some enrichment providers return a confidence score alongside the data.
    Emails marked as "verified" or "valid" have passed a deliverability check.
    Treat unverified emails as risky until validated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Freshness">
    Data goes stale. A LinkedIn URL scraped 18 months ago may point to
    a person who has changed roles. Job titles and company information
    should be treated as approximate, not definitive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Catch-all domains">
    Some company email servers accept all incoming mail regardless of
    whether the address exists (catch-all). These appear valid but
    have high bounce rates. Bitscale flags these — treat them as
    medium-risk and validate before sending at volume.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading Enrichment Columns in Bitscale

When you hover over a cell in an enrichment column, you'll see:
- The raw value returned
- The provider that returned it
- Any validation status (for emails)

If a cell is empty, check the **Errors** view to see why the enrichment
failed for that row — often it's a missing or malformed input value.

## Your Challenge

<Card title="Before moving to F-5" icon="flag-checkered">
  Go back to the grid you built in F-3. For each enrichment column, check:
  (1) what percentage of rows have a value, (2) whether any emails are
  flagged as catch-all or invalid. Note the fill rate and make a list of
  the rows that need better source data.
</Card>
