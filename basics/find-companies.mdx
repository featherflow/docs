## What this workflow is for

Most list building starts with one thing: finding the right companies.

Bitscale makes this easy by letting you:
- search for companies using ICP filters
- import results into a grid
- enrich those companies
- fetch more records in batches once your workflow looks right

## Step 1: Open Find Companies

1. On the top of the platform, click **Find companies**
2. You will see multiple filters to narrow down your ICP

## Step 2: Apply ICP filters

You can mix and match filters depending on how specific you want your search to be.

Common filters include:

### Company keywords
Use this when you are targeting a niche category.

### Company basics
Filter by:
- industry
- business sector

### Size and location
Filter by:
- company headcount
- company location

### Technology
Filter by the tools or technologies a company uses.

### Classification codes
Filter by:
- SIC code
- NAICS code

### Department headcount
This is useful when you care about team composition.

Example: if you sell to sales teams, filter for companies with a larger sales department.

### Funding and revenue
Use this when revenue stage or funding stage matters for your ICP.

1. Click **View results**
2. Review the companies
3. Click **Import to new grid**

If you only need a quick export, you can use the **Download** button to export the first 100 records.

## Step 4: Enrich the companies

Once the companies are in your grid, the next step is usually enrichment.

Bitscale includes an **Enriched company** enrichment that pulls additional company information (like what is available on LinkedIn).

1. Confirm the enrichment is mapped correctly
2. Run it to populate more fields for the companies

## Why the first import is limited to 100 rows

By default, Bitscale brings in the first 100 companies.

This is intentional.

It helps you:
- set up your workflow safely
- test the enrichments
- catch mapping issues early
- avoid burning credits on a large batch before you are confident

Once the workflow is working, you can pull more companies.

## Fetch more records in batches

After you are comfortable with your workflow:

1. Go to **Load data**
2. Click the company filter/source you created
3. Fetch more records in batches (example: 1,000 at a time)
4. Repeat until you run out of search credits or results

## Optional: Set up auto-fetch

If you want a steady flow of companies (example: 100 per week), you can set up auto-fetch and process smaller batches on a schedule.

## Edit or review your filters later

If you ever forget what filters you used:

1. Go to **Load data**
2. Open the saved filter/source
3. View or edit the filters anytime
